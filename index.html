The problem

A communication tool that couldn't communicate.

Velo solves a real pain for small startups: distributed teams falling out of sync. The product works. The mechanism is solid.

But the copy sounded exactly like what it fights — background noise. A system notification. Something that exists, but nobody reads with intent.

The core paradox

"A tool that automates its clients' communication… couldn't communicate itself."

Localization isn't
translation.
It's reframing.

The LATAM market and the English-speaking global market don't just speak different languages. They think in different registers. What converts in Bogotá doesn't convert in San Francisco.

This case study documents that process for what it really is: a strategic decision on every line, not a translation task.

What this demonstrates

Strategic copy.
Not just writing.

🎯
Strategy before writing
Every piece started with a diagnosis. The brief, the paradox, the audience insight — all documented before writing the first line.
🔁
Every decision has a reason
Nothing in this case study is arbitrary. Every annotation explains the why behind the what. That's what separates a strategist from a writer.
🌎
Localization as strategy
ES and EN aren't translations of each other. They're two campaigns with two different mindsets for the same product.
